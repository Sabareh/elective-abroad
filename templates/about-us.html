Why Choose The Elective Abroad Program?

The Elective Abroad Program offers immediate exposure to medicine for individuals who either wish to begin a career in medicine or are looking at enhancing their skill-sets towards specific career lines. Through this program, students discover a realistic view and a better comprehension of practices within this industry. The program is considered one of the most popular options of its kind based on the satisfaction and overall experience of previous participants. These testimonials are presented on the website for the program and extend to those found in Tanzania, Uganda, Philippines, and Kenya.

What to Expect?

The program offers real-life exposure of the medical field. It emphasizes exactly what it means to become a doctor and what it takes to excel in such a career. This provides aspiring students with a well-informed option and allows them to determine whether the industry is right for them.

You will see healthcare delivery in a whole new setting provided through a foreign country. The program introduces you to the truly limited resources of these countries, that although they are limited you see the tremendous need for further development in the healthcare field. Premed students such as yourself are vastly appreciated in the hospitals and by the patients within these regions. This new discovery will lead you to a richer experience overall.

What Does The Program Offer?

It demonstrates several issues from the seriousness of the intention to applying for medical school to one's ability to remain competitive towards a career of choice. It offers recommendations for how to complete your application and enter medical school successfully. Once you discover the program for yourself and realize its importance, you may return to complete your required electives through the program after entering medical school. As you will learn whether you are a non-traditional or premed student the program enhances greatly the methods utilized to enter medical school. It additionally provides you with a full comprehension of the career for which you are aspiring.

Cultural Exchange

The program provides culture exchange and allows you to comprehend truly the needs of different countries. Whether your preferred medical school is located in the states, Europe, or other geographical regions, you will gain invaluable preparation skills. It is this skill set that will allow you to set yourself apart and become more distinguished within the personal statement section of your medical school application.

For those interested in the pre-college program, you learn that the prerequisite for admissions to medical schools in the United States is to volunteer in local clinics and hospitals. This allows you to acquire practical experience within the healthcare industry. Studying abroad, however, requires various prerequisites. You will gain a better understanding of these requirements through participation in the program designed for your preferred geographical region.

What Clinical Fields Are Included?

Exposure to a wealth of clinical fields sets this program apart from the herd. Among the fields, you will observe are:

  • Pediatrics
  • Obstetrics and Gynecology
  • Emergency Medicine and Critical Care
  • Orthopedic Surgery
  • E.N.T.
  • Ophthalmology
  • Radiology

Procedures You Observe In These Fields:

  • Injection Administration
  • Lumbar Punctures
  • Cannulae Intravenous Insertion
  • Nasogastric Intubation
  • Lymph Node Biopsies
  • Orthopedic Surgeries and Procedures
  • Gynecological Procedures: Deliveries, Tubal Ligations, and Cesarean Sections

Within the program, you are taken through ward rotations, which provide you with real-life exposure and integrating theoretical knowledge. It includes observations of the supervising doctors and their consultants to enable you to comprehend the diversity of patients and the care required. This diversity exists within physical examination, body systems, and relevant history pertaining to treatment. You see firsthand how germane laboratory investigations and interpretation affect the pertinent diagnosis and treatment measures that follow.

Who Are The Mentors?

The mentors within the program were hand-selected based on evaluations and quality. They received high regard among previous program participants. These assigned mentors are typically physicians. However, in some countries such as Africa, it is midwives or nurses who perform a large portion of the treatments and services primarily provided by physicians in the United States, for example. With this in mind, your assigned mentor may, in fact, be one of these medical professionals instead of a doctor.

Students within the program discover that nurses perform consultations, provide prescriptions, and admit patients into the medical facilities. However, in some countries, they also perform critical surgeries and administer anesthesia. With your assigned mentor, you acquire exposure to medical experiences by shadowing them throughout their day-to-day operations. They are excellent teachers and offer every effort to provide you with hands-on experience in your chosen field. You are allowed to participate in procedures through close supervision. However, this possibility is limited based on time allotted during the program.

How to Apply?

Submit an application through the program of your choice within your preferred geographical region. Applications do not require fees or any cost to you. A representative from your selected program will notify you via email when details are available. All requirements are sent to you in the same manner once you are accepted.
